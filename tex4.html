Proof of lemma 3:$$ $$ Say \(f(x)\) has a max. value \(M\) Then $$ \begin{aligned} &\left(\int_{0}^{1}(f(x))^{n} d x\right)^{\frac{1}{n}} \\ \leq &\left(\int_{0}^{1} M^{n} d x\right)^{\frac{1}{n}}=M \end{aligned} $$ Since \(f(x)\) is continuous, given \(\epsilon>0\) there exists a \(\delta\) such that $$ f(x)>M-\epsilon $$ for all \(x \in\left(x_{0}-\delta, x_{0}+\delta\right)\). Hence, we have $$ \begin{array}{c} \int_{0}^{1} f(x)^{n} d x>\int_{x_{0}-\delta}^{x_{0}+\delta} f(x)^{n} d x \\ >\int_{x_{0}-\delta}^{x_{0}+\delta}(M-\epsilon)^{n} d x \\ =(M-\epsilon)^{n} 2 \delta \end{array} $$ Hence for any \(\epsilon>0\) $$ \begin{array}{l} \left(\int_{0}^{1} f(x)^{n} d x\right)^{1 / n} \\ >(M-\epsilon)(2 \delta)^{1 / n} \end{array} $$ Letting \(n \rightarrow \infty\) $$\lim _{n \rightarrow \infty}\left(\int_{0}^{1} f(x)^{n} d x\right)^{1 / n }\geq(M-\epsilon)$$ Fix \(\delta>0\) and let \(S_{\delta}:=\left\{x,|f(x)| \geqslant\|f\|_{\infty}-\delta\right\}\) for \(\delta<\|f\|_{\infty}\). We have $$ \begin{aligned} \|f\|_{p} & \geqslant\left(\int_{S_{\delta}}\left(\|f\|_{\infty}-\delta\right)^{p} d \mu\right)^{1 / p} \\ &=\left(\|f\|_{\infty}-\delta\right) \mu\left(S_{\delta}\right)^{1 / p} \end{aligned} $$ since \(\mu\left(S_{\delta}\right)\) is finite and positive. This gives $$ \liminf _{p \rightarrow+\infty}\|f\|_{p} \geqslant\|f\|_{\infty} $$ As \(|f(x)| \leqslant\|f\|_{\infty}\) for almost every \(x\), we have for \(p>q\), $$ \|f\|_{p} \leqslant\left(\int_{X}|f(x)|^{p-q}|f(x)|^{q} d \mu\right)^{1 / p} \leqslant\|f\|_{\infty}^{\frac{p-q}{p}}\|f\|_{q}^{q / p}, $$ giving the reverse inequality. $$ $$ $$ $$
